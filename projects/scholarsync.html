Why It
Exists

Keyword Limitations

Traditional academic databases (like Google Scholar or local indexes) rely heavily on literal token matching. If a researcher searches for "deep learning weight initialization", the system might completely miss crucial papers that discuss "variance-scaling gradient convergence parameters" because the exact words don't align.

ScholarSync was developed to enable semantic discovery. By mapping unstructured academic documents into dense multidimensional vector spaces, it recognizes conceptual similarities regardless of the specific vocabulary used by authors.

05

Challenges
& Solutions

Context Engineering

Ingesting full research publications (which often exceed 30 pages) poses extreme challenges for model context windows and memory footprints.

Challenge: Token Overload and Long Context Hallucinations

Passing complete papers into the LLM caused context overflows, high latency, and key details to get lost in the middle of long texts.

Fix: Hierarchical Chunking & Cross-Encoder Reranking

We designed a chunking pipeline that slices papers into overlapping 500-token sections tagged by document region (e.g. Abstract, Methods). During query time, a fast cosine-similarity search finds the top 20 chunks, which are then passed to a Cross-Encoder reranking model to filter down to the top 5 most relevant segments for final prompt generation. This reduced LLM noise and context consumption by 80%.
